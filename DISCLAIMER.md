## Computational Predictions Are Not Experimental Results

All metrics generated in the notebooks — including but not limited to **pLDDT scores**, **RMSD values**, **binding affinity estimates**, **pAE scores**, and **self-consistency scores** — are computational predictions produced by machine learning models. These metrics:

- Are approximations, not measurements
- May not correlate with experimental outcomes in all cases
- Should not be cited as evidence of biological function without wet-lab validation
- Are subject to the known limitations and biases of their respective models (AlphaFold, ESMFold, RFDiffusion, ProteinMPNN)

A high pLDDT score does not guarantee that a protein will fold correctly in vitro. A low RMSD does not guarantee that a designed sequence will express or function as intended.
